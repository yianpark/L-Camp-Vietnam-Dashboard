What Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Impact Does Our Solution Address?Our fungi-mycelium technology not only demonstrates a clear superiority over current plant-based solutions on the market by naturally mimicking the fibrous texture of animal meat without heavy ultra-processing or chemical binders, but it also outperforms them in nutritional density and resource efficiency. Validating this claim, comprehensive life-cycle assessments (LCA) indicate that the greenhouse gas emissions (GHGe) of raw mycelium production (approx. 0.73kg CO2 eq/kg) are significantly lower than those of soy protein (1.21kg CO2 eq/kg) and pea protein (1.91kg CO2 eq/kg) (see the systematic review on : https://www.researchgate.net/publication/386295883_The_environmental_impact_of_mycoprotein-based_meat_alternatives_compared_to_plant-based_meat_alternatives_A_systematic_review). Furthermore, from a health perspective, mycoprotein boasts a Protein Digestibility-Corrected Amino Acid Score (PDCAAS) of 96%–97%, outperforming soy (91%), and naturally contains high-fiber structures that aid in cholesterol regulation—benefits that conventional plant-based items struggle to offer simultaneously (see the medical synthesis at the National Institutes of Health - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11743834/). Beyond these technical advantages, our solution directly addresses the critical conflict between growing food demand and environmental degradation by providing a scalable, sustainable, and healthier alternative protein. Environmental (E)Massive Carbon Reduction: Our mycelium-based protein has a carbon footprint 90% to 98% lower than traditional beef. To date, by our directly and indirectly measuring, our solution has successfully reduced 72 million metric tons of CO2 gas emissions. Resource Efficiency: Our proprietary fungal strains can grow 100 times within just 10 days, requiring significantly less land, water, and time compared to traditional livestock farming. This resource optimization is backed by international scientific consensus showing that filamentous fungi cultivation requires at least 10 times less land and water than animal meat (read the scientific report in the Royal Society of Chemistry Journal: https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlelanding/2024/fb/d3fb00169e). This directly mitigates global land exploitation, deforestation, and habitat loss. Zero-Waste & Clean Food: Our production process completely eliminates the risk of antibiotic resistance common in animal agriculture and champions eco-friendly food tech innovation. Social (S)Our commitment to social impact is strictly validated by international standards, achieving an outstanding Overall Impact Score of 8.8 out of 10 verified by the Impact Investment Exchange (IIX): Inclusivity & Employment: We have directly and indirectly created sustainable jobs and increased income for over 3,000 disabled individuals, vulnerable women, and unprivileged communities. Farmer Support: We actively integrate and support more than 530 local farmers in the agricultural mushroom supply chain. Community Education & Welfare: We have trained and coached over 1,000 students and suppliers on green business, farming, and climate change. Additionally, our philanthropic initiatives have supported 3,000 homeless individuals/children and helped over 2,000 highland minority children access education. Governance (G)Our solution operates under strict transparency, compliant with global sustainability frameworks including IRIS+, SDG Impact, and the Impact Management Project (IMP). Emmay는 균사체(mycoprotein) 기반 기술로 식물성 단백질 대비 우수한 맛·식감·영양을 주장하나, 블라인드 테스트, 제3자 관능평가, 재구매율 데이터가 [미확인]이다. 30만 최종 소비자 확보는 [확인]되나, 채널별 이탈률·리텐션·NPS 등 실질적 제품 경쟁력 지표 부재로 소비자 선호도의 지속 가능성을 판단하기 어렵다. 베트남 시장 내 경쟁사(Vbites, Green Rebel, Karana) 대비 가격 경쟁력과 SKU 수는 언급되나, 시장점유율·브랜드 인지도·소비자 전환 비용 분석 [미확인]이다. 기술적 우위가 실제 시장 우위로 전환되는 메커니즘에 대한 구체적 검증 필요.
